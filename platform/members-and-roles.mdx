---
title: "Members and roles"
description: "Invite team members and assign roles at the organization and chatbot level"
---

OpenBat has two levels of member management: **organization-level** (applies to all chatbots) and **chatbot-level** (access to a specific chatbot).

## Organization members

Manage organization members at `/platform/members`. The table shows each member's email, role, and join date.

### Invite a member

<Steps>
  <Step title="Click Invite member">
    Click the **+ Invite member** button on the members page.

    <Frame>
      <img src="/docs/state-screenshots/state-platform-members-invite.png" alt="Invite member modal with email field and role dropdown" />
    </Frame>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enter their email">
    Type the new member's email address.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select a role">
    Choose a role from the dropdown:

    <Frame>
      <img src="/docs/state-screenshots/state-platform-members-role-dropdown.png" alt="Role dropdown showing Admin and Member options" />
    </Frame>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Send the invitation">
    Click **Invite**. The member receives an email invitation and appears in the table once they accept.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Roles

<Accordion title="Owner">
  Full access to all chatbots and settings. Can rename and delete the organization, manage members, and perform all admin actions. Assigned to the organization creator.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Admin">
  Can manage chatbots and invite new members. Has access to all chatbot dashboards, settings, and configurations within the organization.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Member">
  Read-only access. Can view dashboards, conversations, and analytics but cannot modify settings or invite members.
</Accordion>

## Chatbot members

Individual chatbots can have their own member list, managed from `/platform/{chatbotId}/settings` > **Members** tab.

<Frame>
  <img src="/docs/state-screenshots/state-chatbot-settings-members.png" alt="Chatbot members tab showing member table and Add member button" />
</Frame>

Click **+ Add member** to give a specific user access to this chatbot. Chatbot-level access is useful when you want to grant someone access to a single chatbot without adding them to the entire organization.

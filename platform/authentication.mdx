OpenBat supports email/password authentication and social sign-in with Apple, Google, and Meta.

## Sign up

Create an account at `/auth/sign-up`:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Enter your email">
    Provide a valid email address.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Create a password">
    Enter a password with at least 8 characters.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Confirm your password">
    Re-enter your password to confirm.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Click Create Account">
    Submit the form. You may receive a verification email — check your inbox and click the link to confirm your account. After verification, you're redirected to the platform to create your first organization.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Warning>
  The sign-up form requires a password of at least 8 characters. Always use 8 or more characters for security.
</Warning>

You can also sign up using Apple, Google, or Meta by clicking the corresponding social auth button.

## Log in

Sign in at `/auth/login` with your email and password, or use a social provider.

The login page features a split layout with the form on the left and a decorative image on the right.

### Email login

Enter your email and password, then click **Login**. On success, you're redirected to the dashboard at `/platform`.

### Social login

Click **Login with Apple**, **Login with Google**, or **Login with Meta** to authenticate via OAuth. Complete the provider's flow and you'll be redirected back to the dashboard.

## Reset your password

If you've forgotten your password:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Go to the login page">
    Navigate to `/auth/login`.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Click Forgot your password">
    Click the **Forgot your password?** link below the login form.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enter your email">
    On the password reset page at `/auth/forgot-password`, enter the email address associated with your account.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Check your email">
    Click **Send reset email**. Check your inbox for a link to set a new password.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  If you don't see the reset email, check your spam folder. Reset links expire after a set period.
</Note>

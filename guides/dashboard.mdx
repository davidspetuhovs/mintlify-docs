The dashboard is where you monitor your chatbot's health at a glance. It surfaces sentiment trends, behavior issues, and revenue signals from your conversations.

![Dashboard overview](/images/dashboard.png)

## Overview strip

Four stat cards are displayed across the top of the dashboard:

- **Conversations** — total count for the selected period
- **Messages** — total messages analyzed
- **Avg sentiment** — average sentiment score with a trend indicator (positive, neutral, or negative)
- **Analyzed today** — messages analyzed in the current day

## Controls

**Date range picker** — choose from presets (Today, This week, This month, Last 30 days) or set a custom range. All dashboard data updates to reflect the selected period.

**Segment filter** — filter all dashboard data by customer plan (e.g., Free, Pro, Enterprise). This applies to every chart and table on both tabs.

## User insights

The default tab. It shows how your users feel, what they talk about, and where they struggle.

![User insights](/images/dashboard-insights.png)

<Accordion title="Sentiment trend">
  Time-series chart showing user satisfaction over the selected period. Spot drops in sentiment early and correlate them with product changes or incidents.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Top user frustrations">
  Table of recurring pain points extracted from conversations, ranked by frequency. Shows what users complain about most so you can prioritize fixes.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Most dissatisfied users">
  Ranked list of users with the lowest sentiment scores. Each entry has an "Investigate" link that takes you to their filtered conversations on the [conversations page](/guides/conversations).
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Support topics">
  Auto-discovered topic tags from conversations. Shows what your users are actually talking about, without any manual tagging.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Revenue signals">
  Three sub-sections surface commercial insights from your conversations:

  - **Churn risk** — users showing signs of leaving
  - **Buying signals** — users interested in upgrades or new plans
  - **Capability gaps** — features users ask about that the bot cannot handle

  See [revenue signals](/concepts/revenue-signals) for details on how these are detected.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Top intents">
  Chart of the most common user intents — what users are trying to do when they message your bot.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Sentiment by segment">
  Breakdown of sentiment across customer plans. Compare how Free vs Pro vs Enterprise users feel about your bot's responses.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Activity heatmap">
  Shows when your users are most active, broken down by day of week and time of day. Useful for planning maintenance windows and staffing live support.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Languages">
  Breakdown of conversation languages detected. See which languages your users write in and whether sentiment varies across them.
</Accordion>

## Assistant performance

The second tab. It focuses on how your bot behaves and whether it actually resolves user problems.

<Accordion title="Behavior alerts">
  Table of detected bot behaviors with severity levels. Four behavior types are tracked:

  - **Hallucinating** — bot generating false or unsupported information
  - **Dodging** — bot avoiding the user's actual question
  - **Verbose** — bot giving unnecessarily long responses
  - **Redirecting** — bot sending users elsewhere instead of answering

  Each row shows the behavior type, occurrence count, and severity level. See [behavior detection](/concepts/behavior-detection) for details on how these are identified.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Resolution outcomes">
  Donut chart breaking down how conversations end: Resolved, Deflected, or Partially Resolved. Track this over time to measure whether your bot is improving.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Quality flags">
  Issues detected in assistant responses, such as hallucination risk or sensitive data exposure. These flags highlight specific response-level problems.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Capability gaps">
  Features and topics users ask about that the bot cannot handle. These are unmet needs your product team should know about — they represent opportunities to expand your bot's coverage.
</Accordion>

## Investigating a user

When you see a dissatisfied user in the "Most dissatisfied users" list, click "Investigate" next to their name. This navigates to the [conversations page](/guides/conversations) filtered by that user's email, showing all their interactions so you can understand what went wrong.

<Tip>
  Combine the dashboard with [workflows](/guides/workflows) to get automatic alerts when sentiment drops or behaviors spike — so you do not have to watch the dashboard manually.
</Tip>

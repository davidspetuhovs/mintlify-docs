---
title: "Deep search"
description: "Search conversations by meaning, not keywords. Find what users are talking about even when they don't use the exact words you expect."
---

Deep search lets you find conversations by what they mean, not what they literally say. Search for "users frustrated with billing" and you'll find conversations about payment issues — even if nobody actually said the word "billing."

![Deep search](/images/deep-search.png)

## How it works

Deep search uses semantic matching to understand the intent behind your query and find relevant conversations. Unlike keyword search, it understands synonyms, related concepts, and implied meaning.

## Using deep search

<Steps>
  <Step title="Navigate to Deep Search">
    Navigate to the **Deep Search** page from the sidebar.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Set a date range">
    Optionally set a **date range** to narrow your search window.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Enter your query">
    Type a natural language query describing what you're looking for.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Run the search">
    Click **Deep Search**.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Review results">
    Review the results, ranked by semantic relevance.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Example queries

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="users frustrated with billing">
    Finds conversations about payment confusion, invoice issues, or pricing complaints.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="bot gave wrong information">
    Finds conversations where the assistant provided incorrect answers, even if flagged differently.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="users asking about features we don't have">
    Surfaces capability gap conversations.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="complaints about slow response time">
    Finds conversations about performance and latency issues.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="users comparing us to competitors">
    Finds competitive intelligence buried in support chats.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="conversations where the bot couldn't help">
    Finds deflected or unresolved interactions.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Tips for effective searches

<Tip>
  - Be specific about intent rather than using single words
  - Describe the situation you're looking for in plain language
  - Use the date range filter to focus on a specific time period
  - Combine deep search with [conversation detail](/guides/conversations) to review individual results
</Tip>

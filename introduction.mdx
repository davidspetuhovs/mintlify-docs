---
title: "What is OpenBat?"
description: "OpenBat is analytics for AI chatbots. It reads every conversation and shows you what's going wrong — hallucinations, user frustration, churn risk, and more."
---

Your users are frustrated, your bot is hallucinating, and it's all buried in chat logs nobody reads. OpenBat reads every message — both sides — and shows you what's actually going wrong. It catches the problems you didn't know you had and surfaces them before your users churn.

![OpenBat dashboard overview](/images/landing.png)

## Key capabilities

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Conversation analysis" icon="comments">
    Automatically analyze every message for sentiment, intent, topics, and flags. See what users are asking about and how your bot responds — across every conversation.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Behavior detection" icon="robot">
    Catch hallucinations, dodging, verbose responses, and redirecting before users complain. Every assistant message gets flagged with behavior badges so nothing slips through.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Revenue signals" icon="chart-line">
    Surface churn risk, buying signals, and capability gaps from conversation patterns. Know which users are about to leave and which ones are ready to upgrade.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Workflow automation" icon="bolt">
    Set up alerts that fire when specific conditions are detected. Route notifications to Slack, email, or webhooks so the right person acts on them immediately.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Deep search" icon="magnifying-glass">
    Find conversations by meaning, not keywords. Ask "users frustrated with billing" and get results even if nobody actually said "billing."
  </Card>
  <Card title="Multi-language support" icon="globe">
    Analyze conversations in any language with automatic translation. Your users write in their language — OpenBat understands all of them.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## Who uses OpenBat

**Customer success managers** — Track user satisfaction across accounts, investigate dissatisfied users, and monitor organization health over time.

**Product managers** — Understand what users ask about most, identify capability gaps in your bot, and track sentiment trends across releases.

**Engineering leads** — Catch bot behavior issues early, monitor hallucination rates, and track whether conversations actually resolve user problems.

**Prompt engineers** — See exactly where prompts fail in real conversations. Review behavior badges on every assistant message to find patterns and fix them.
